Andersonville

Andersonville, a tiny neighborhood on Chicago's far North Side that straddles the border between Edgewater and Uptown, is most known for its vibrant Clark Street strip, its LGBTQ community, and a water tower painted with a Swedish flag that stands atop a museum. Learn More...

Edgewater

Edgewater is a lakefront neighborhood area on Chicago's North Side, Illinois, six miles north of the Loop. Edgewater, the city's final formal community area, is limited on the south by Foster Avenue, on the north by Devon Avenue, on the west by Ravenswood Avenue, and on the east by Lake Michigan. Gladstone Park

Gladstone Park is located in the Gladstone neighborhood (between Milwaukee Avenue and Northwest Highway, and between Central and Austin Avenues). A small fieldhouse, a t-ball field and junior soccer field, a new ADA accessible soft-surface playground and spray pool, and a tiny outdoor basketball court are all available at the park. Jefferson Park

Jefferson Park is bounded to the northwest by Norwood Park, to the northeast by Forest Glen, to the south by Portage Park, and to the west by the Harwood Heights neighborhood. The Guatemalan consulate is situated at 5559 North Elston Avenue in Jefferson Park. Learn More...

North Park

North Park is one of the City of Chicago's 77 distinct community zones. It is bounded on the east by the North Shore Channel, on the south by the Chicago River's North Branch and Foster Avenue, on the west by Cicero Avenue (except for the section in the northwest corner, north of Bryn Mawr Avenue, which is part of the Sauganash neighborhood), and on the north by Devon Avenue.
