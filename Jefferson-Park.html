Jefferson Park

About

Jefferson Park is one of Chicago's 77 community areas, situated on the city's Northwest Side. The Jefferson Park neighborhood encompasses a greater area.

Jefferson Park is bounded to the northwest by Norwood Park, to the northeast by Forest Glen, to the south by Portage Park, and to the west by the Harwood Heights neighborhood. The Guatemalan consulate is situated at 5559 North Elston Avenue in Jefferson Park.

History

Settlement in the Jefferson Park area began in the 1830s with John Kinzie Clark and Elijah Wentworth, who had a claim near what is now the Jefferson Park Metra Station and ran a tavern and inn. Until Abram Gale, after whom Gale Street is called, erected the first frame house in Jefferson, the modest village of traders, hunters, and farmers consisted of basic one and two room log houses. Jefferson Park became the center of an autonomous township that was founded as Jefferson Township in 1850 at the adjacent Dickinson Tavern and was annexed by the city of Chicago in 1889. The region formerly had a sizable Volga German community, and one of the area's former local monuments was a local apartment building near the park along Higgins Avenue nicknamed by residents as "the Russian Hotel."

The Northwest Chicago Historical Society, which is devoted to preserving the area's rich history, is also housed at Jefferson Park, as are most historical events and lectures. A Boutique Air Pilatus PC-12 was arriving at Chicago O'Hare Airport on January 21, 2021 when its left main tire detached and landed in Jefferson Park. The tire and other minor components were discovered on a sidewalk near Leland Avenue, according to police. The tire collided with two homes before coming to a stop on the pavement.

Education

Residents of Jefferson Park are serviced by Chicago Public Schools, which offers both neighborhood and citywide alternatives for kids. There are also a number of private religious schools in the region maintained by Roman Catholic and Lutheran communities. The Jefferson Park branch of the Chicago Public Library serves the citizens of the area.


Transportation

Jefferson Park has long been a transit center in Chicago, giving the area the moniker "The Gateway to Chicago." A Blue Line stop in the Kennedy Expressway median at the junction of Milwaukee and Gale Street, less than three blocks from the Copernicus Center and the historic Jefferson Park Congregational Church, serves the area. Jefferson Park is also served by the Union Pacific / Northwest Line. A statue to Thomas Jefferson was erected along Milwaukee Avenue near the station's entrance in 2005.
